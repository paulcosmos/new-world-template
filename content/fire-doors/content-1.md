## What **exactly** do fire doors do?

Fire doors save lives. They act as barriers against the rapid spread of flames and smoke during a fire incident. They are constructed using materials that have been tested and certified (rated) to meet specific fire resistance standards. This means that they can withstand high temperatures for a specified period of time, typically ranging from 30 minutes to several hours.

The importance of fire doors cannot be overstated when it comes to safeguarding lives and property. In the event of a fire, these doors serve as vital compartments that help contain the flames within a specific area or room. By doing so, they create safe escape routes for individuals while preventing the rapid spread of heat and toxic gases throughout the building.
